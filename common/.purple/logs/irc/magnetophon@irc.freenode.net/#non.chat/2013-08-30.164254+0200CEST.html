(12:33:37 PM) magnetophon: male: Another thing: now that you've taken out the lp, there will be no more backward compatibility in the spatializer. At least not without some kind of session file version checking. IIRC that leaves just one argument for leaving the lp where it is: reminding the user that their signal might be too bassy. The arguments against are, in order of importance: 1: even in those cases where you'd need a hp, you'd normally want it before any compressors and limiters. 2: it has conceptually nothing to do with the spatializer, except that both are processes you'd often employ on close mics. (but the spatializer will be used on a much wider category of sources: synths, samples, DI's, close mics with an analog hp...) 3: it takes screen reel-estate. 4: a separate hp will give you a choice of slopes (at least the one I use does!). So the hp is there to educate your users, but the end effect would be you'd be teaching them (somewhat) the wrong thing, due to point 1 and to a lesser extent 2 and 4. No biggie either way, but as long as you're giving up backwards compatibility anyway, you might as well reconsider some other things.... IMHO :p
